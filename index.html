What is Indie Animation

Indie animation refers to animated content that is created independently, outside of major studio systems, often by small teams or even individual creators. These projects are typically self-funded or supported through crowdfunding platforms like Patreon or Kickstarter, and they thrive on platforms such as YouTube, Vimeo, and streaming services that support creator-driven content. What sets indie animation apart is its creative freedom—unlike mainstream productions, indie creators are not bound by corporate guidelines, allowing them to explore bold, unconventional stories, experiment with unique visual styles, and represent diverse voices and experiences. From comedic shorts to full-length series, indie animation spans every genre imaginable, often blending heartfelt storytelling with innovative visuals.

Hidden Gems You Should Watch

Some standout indie animation shows include "Hazbin Hotel" by VivziePop, "Helluva Boss" by SpindleHorse Toons, and "Meta Runner" by Glitch Productions. These shows not only showcase amazing animation but also demonstrate the power of independent creators to build dedicated fanbases, a few, like Hazbin Hotel, and Long Gone Glutch, and The Amazing Digital Circus being picked up and streamed by major networks, like Amazon, Disney, and even Netflix!

Hazbin Hotel

Hazbin Hotel is a musical dark comedy set in Hell, created by Vivienne Medrano (VivziePop). It follows Charlie, the princess of Hell, as she tries to rehabilitate sinners and give them a second chance. With vibrant animation and Broadway-level songs, it quickly became a viral hit and a landmark in indie animation.

Helluva Boss

Set in the same universe as Hazbin Hotel, Helluva Boss tells a totally different story—this time focusing on the employees of a chaotic assassination company in Hell. It’s raunchy, ridiculous, and often surprisingly emotional. The show is completely self-funded and made with love by the same team as Hazbin.

Lackadasiy

Based on the webcomic by Tracy Butler, Lackadaisy is a gorgeously animated Prohibition-era story about bootlegging cats with jazz, drama, and gunfights. Its pilot blew viewers away with its stunning visuals and detailed animation, all produced by a team of indie artists and fans through crowdfunding.

Murder Drones

Created by Glitch Productions and directed by Liam Vickers, Murder Drones is a sci-fi horror-comedy about sassy robot teens surviving in a dystopian world where murder is a daily routine. It’s sleek, weird, and full of unexpected heart—proving how indie animation can tackle complex genres and still be fun.

Long Gluch Gone

This wild-west fantasy adventure is the brainchild of Tara Billinger and Zach Bellissimo. Long Gone Gulch mixes old-school cartoons with modern energy, creating a bizarre town full of monsters, cowboys, and magical chaos. It’s a perfect example of indie creators doing whatever the heck they want—and making it look great.

The Amazing Digital Circus

From the mind of Gooseworx and produced by Glitch Productions, The Amazing Digital Circus is a neon-colored fever dream set inside a virtual world. It’s weird, whimsical, unsettling, and impossible to look away from. With its surreal humor and eerie undertones, it's a new favorite in the indie scene.
